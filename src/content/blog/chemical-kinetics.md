The rate of a chemical reaction increases with the increase of temperature. This is described by the Arrhenius eqaution.

In the above equation, A is the Arrhenius factor or the frequency factor or the pre-exponential factor. It is specific to a given reation. R is the gas constant and Ea is the activation energy measured in joules/mol. The fraction Ea/RT denotes the fraction of molecules which have the energy greater than the activation energy, the energy required to make a activated complex.
